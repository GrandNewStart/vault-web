Digital Signatures

Overview

What is a Digital Signature?

A digital signature is a cryptographic mechanism used to verify the **authenticity** and **integrity** of digital messages or documents. It is the digital equivalent of a handwritten signature or a wax seal, but infinitely more secure.

Think of it like a **wax seal combined with a signet ring**:

  1. Only you possess the unique signet ring (Private Key) to stamp the seal.
  2. Anyone can compare the stamped seal against your public crest (Public Key) to verify it came from you.
  3. If anyone attempts to alter the message inside the envelope, the wax seal breaks (Integrity Verification).

Security Properties

The Three Cryptographic Guarantees

Authentication: Confirms who signed the message. Because only you have the Private Key, no one else could have created the signature.
Integrity: Proves that the message has not been altered in transit. If even a single byte is changed, the signature verification immediately fails.
Non-Repudiation: Prevents the signer from claiming they did not send the message, as their unique Private Key was required to generate the signature.
